Tectonic Plates and Natural Disasters in Colombia

Colombia lies at the intersection of the South American Plate and the Nazca Plate. This convergent boundary causes significant geological activity, including earthquakes and volcanic eruptions. For example, the 1999 Armenia earthquake (Mw 6.2) devastated parts of western Colombia. The country also lies near the Caribbean Plate, contributing to seismic risk in the north.

Rock and Mineral Resources

Colombia is rich in minerals like gold, emeralds, coal, and nickel. These resources contribute significantly to exports, especially emeralds, which make up over 90% of the global supply. Coal is exported to Europe and Asia. However, mining has environmental impacts including deforestation and water pollution.

Earthquake Activity

Significant earthquakes in Colombia include the 1983 Popayán earthquake and the 1999 Armenia quake. The Colombian Geological Service monitors seismic activity and promotes earthquake preparedness programs, including early warning systems and structural building codes in high-risk zones.

Volcanoes of Colombia

Major volcanoes include Nevado del Ruiz (composite), Galeras (active stratovolcano), and Puracé. Nevado del Ruiz erupted in 1985, triggering a deadly lahar that killed 23,000 people in Armero. Today, these volcanoes are monitored for risk while also serving as tourist attractions and contributing to fertile farmland.

Major Mountain Ranges

Colombia is traversed by the Andes, which split into three branches: the Western, Central, and Eastern Cordilleras. These ranges affect climate (rainfall in valleys, snow in peaks), influence agriculture, and form cultural barriers. Historically, they limited internal trade and protected regions from conquest.
